Books On Our Shelf

Four books for understanding the civilizations Hidden Epoch investigates — Sumer, Babylon, the Hittites, and the wider Ancient Near East. Primary scholarship, not pop history. Source material we actually use.

Start Here · Babylon

Babylon: Mesopotamia and the Birth of Civilization

Paul Kriwaczek · 2012

3,000 years of Mesopotamia in one readable volume. Argues that almost everything we call "civilization" — cities, writing, law, banking, the seven-day week — was invented between the Tigris and the Euphrates.

Sumer

The Sumerians: Their History, Culture, and Character

Samuel Noah Kramer · 1971

Kramer is the man who taught the world to read Sumerian. He translated the original tablets the rest of the field still argues over — including the flood story that predates Genesis by 1,500 years.

Get on Amazon →
Hittites

The Kingdom of the Hittites

Trevor Bryce · 2006 · Oxford

Definitive academic history of the empire that was lost for 3,000 years and only confirmed in the early 1900s — even though the Bible had been naming the Hittites the whole time.

Get on Amazon →
Survey · Ancient Near East

The Ancient Near East: A History

William W. Hallo & William K. Simpson · 1997

The single best one-volume textbook for getting your bearings across the entire region — Sumer, Akkad, Babylon, Assyria, Egypt, the Hittites, Persia. The frame to hang the rest on.
